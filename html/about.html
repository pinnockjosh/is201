The Distinctive History of San Francisco

Founded in 1776 by Spanish colonists, San Francisco was a mission before experiencing rapid growth during the Gold Rush of 1849. Almost one-quarter of the California population lived in the city between 1870 and 1900. This growth, however, was devastated by the 1906 earthquake and fire, which destroyed three-quarters of the city.

The city was quickly rebuilt and became a port of embarkation for naval service members serving in the Pacific area. The United Nations was born there in 1945. A snowballing effect of liberalizing attitudes, significant immigration, and more cemented San Francisco as a center of liberal activism in the United States.
